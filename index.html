From vehicle dynamics to autonomous driving

I'm a robotics graduate student at the University of Michigan with a vehicle engineering background. I build things that move: FSAE suspension and drivetrain hardware, FEA-verified structures, and perception pipelines for ADAS. My focus is motion planning and autonomy for road vehicles.

I started on the mechanical side — a B.S. in Vehicle Engineering at National Taipei University of Technology (GPA 3.6/4.0), a year on the Taipei Tech Racing Formula SAE team, and a thesis applying FEA structural and fatigue analysis. Internships at Foxconn's commercial EV division and iPE showed me how vehicles get engineered at production scale — and how much of the industry's future depends on software. That pulled me toward ADAS: computer-vision lane detection, deep-learning perception, and vehicle-dynamics simulation in CarSim and BikeSim. Long term, I want to do R&D at the intersection of vehicle dynamics and autonomy.
