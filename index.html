01 — about

Mechanical roots, autonomy trajectory

I started on the mechanical side of vehicles: a B.S. in Vehicle Engineering at National Taipei University of Technology, a year on the NTUT Formula SAE team designing suspension geometry and manufacturing drivetrain components, and a thesis applying FEA structural and fatigue analysis.

Internships at Foxconn's commercial EV division and iPE (wheel product development) showed me how vehicles get engineered at production scale — and how much of the industry's future depends on software. That pulled me toward ADAS: computer-vision lane detection, deep-learning perception, and vehicle-dynamics simulation in CarSim and BikeSim.

At Michigan Robotics I'm building the estimation, planning, and control foundation to work on autonomous vehicles end-to-end. Long term, I want to do R&D at the intersection of vehicle dynamics and autonomy.

Medical Image Segmentation & Detection — AI CUP 2025

DEEP LEARNING

Built two 3D medical-imaging pipelines for cardiac CT: a UNet3D model for whole-heart segmentation and a YOLOv12 detector for aortic-valve localization, covering the full workflow under competition constraints.

  • Segmentation: volumetric UNet3D on cardiac CT — data preprocessing, augmentation, training, and post-processing for whole-heart masks.
  • Detection: YOLOv12 for aortic-valve localization, tuned for the small-target, low-contrast regime of medical imagery.
  • Takeaway: end-to-end ownership of a deep-learning pipeline — the same skill set behind camera-based ADAS perception.
